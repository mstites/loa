Project Overview

The primary project during my LOA is to design and prototype sex toys with trans feminine pleasure at the forefront. This project is part of a long term interest I have had in gender affirming sex and pleasure.

Why is this important?

In the current adult market very little consideration is paid to trans pleasure while designing sex toys. This is especially true for trans feminine people who sit at the bottom of the pleasure power structure due to the intersections of transphobia and misogyny.

My work will focus on making toys for trans feminine people with penises. The existing toys for penises on the market are entirely designed for cis men. This is problematic for many trans feminine people for two main reasons:

  1. The ways the toys are designed to be used is not affirming and/or dysphoria inducing.
  2. Many trans feminine people take hormones (HRT). HRT can cause a number of changes in the penis (eg higher sensitivity, softer, less/no erections) which can make toys designed for cis men's penises not pleasurable and/or painful to use.

This leaves transfeminine people with penises designed specifically for their stimulation. There is always the option of penetrative play or using vibrators that can work on everyone (eg the magic wand). But transfeminine people deserve access to toys specifically made for them, just as people with vulvas can have clitoral and cis men can have penile toys made specifically for them.

Project Outcome

Deliverable

The final goal of my project is to have a working, refined, prototype (or prototypes). In the future, I would like to work towards producing toys at scale and at a price that is accessible for trans feminine people.

Learning Goals

A major focus of this project is personal growth and learning. My learning goals are:

  • Self-directed learning and project management skills
  • Using creative, efficient, and effective (goals orientated) prototyping methods
  • Making body safe silicone toys
  • The idea to product pipeline
  • The intersection of art and engineering
  • Queer and trans sexuality and pleasure
